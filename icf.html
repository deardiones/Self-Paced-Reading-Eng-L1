INFORMED CONSENT FORM

 

Dear Mr./Mrs.,

 

My name is Dionatan Bastos Cardozo, a Ph.D. student at the Federal University of Santa Catarina in Brazil, conducting research at the University of Toronto under the guidance of Professor Suzi Lima, Ph.D. in Linguistics. I would like to invite you to participate in the research project titled "Semantic Variation and Influence in the Nominal System from Brazilian Portuguese (L1) to English (L2): An Experimental Study."

 

Why is this research being conducted?

 

This study focuses on the interpretation of nouns in Brazilian Portuguese and English. The study aims to explore how grammatical differences between Brazilian Portuguese and English may impact the acquisition of English as a Second Language by native speakers of Brazilian Portuguese.

 

What it is going to happen?

 

We will meet once for up to 40 minutes. If you agree to participate in the study, kindly click on the checkbox at the end of this page. A digital copy of this Informed Consent Form can be provided to you upon request. Following that, you will:

  1. Answer a profile questionnaire.

And then participate in one of the two tasks described as follows:

  1. Engage in a task where you will read a sentence word by word on a computer screen and assess the interpretation of the sentence at the end of your reading. Two geometrical figures will display on your screen, and you should click on the one you believe best represents the interpretation of the sentence.
  2. Participate in another task within the study, which involves reading sentences and rating them on a scale of 1 to 5 based on how good the sentence is to you.

 

What are the risks?

 

The risks are minimal. Participants may experience discomfort, frustration, irritability, and/or fatigue during the tasks. If that happens, they can inform the accompanying researcher, take a break to drink water, and/or discontinue their participation.

 

Are there any benefits?

 

There are no direct benefits. However, the findings of this study can contribute to the development of the field, both for future research and for supporting foreign language teaching in the classroom. In addition, students will be compensated with course credits (usually 1~2%) if they are recruited from the Department of Linguistics participant pool. If they are not recruited from the pool of participants who are doing studies for credits in courses, they will be paid 10 dollars for their participation in the study, which lasts 40 minutes.

 

 

Will the identity of the participants be disclosed in future outcomes of this project?

 

Under no circumstances will the identity of the participants be disclosed. All information will be kept confidential, and participants will be identified solely by a random sequence of letters and numbers assigned by a computer program. While every effort will be made to maintain the confidentiality of participant names, I acknowledge the potential risk of a breach. The testing platform will generate a random alphanumeric code to identify each participant, and this code should be retained by the participant.

 

Is the researcher going to be in the room?

 

Yes, throughout the data collection process, I will be present. Any questions can be addressed to me before, during, or after the study is concluded, by email.

 

Is participation mandatory?

 

No, your participation is entirely voluntary and you will not be financially compensated. This document is an invitation. If you prefer to not participate in this task, you will not be affected in any way. You can just close the web browser.

 

Is it possible to withdraw from participating in this study?

 

Yes, it is possible to withdraw from participating in this study. Please send an email to dionatan.cardozo@mail.utoronto.ca. I can delete your data if you share the code generated by the system when you completed the study.

 

If you have any additional questions, you can ask them during the study or by email.
